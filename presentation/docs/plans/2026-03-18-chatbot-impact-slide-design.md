# Chatbot Impact Slide Design

**Goal:** Align `slides/3.html` and `slides/4.html` into a connected story: slide 3 explains the deployment background for customer-support AI, and slide 4 shows the measured impact with a small source caption.

**Context:**
- `slides/3.html` currently presents generic problem-definition cards.
- `slides/4.html` now presents chatbot impact metrics.
- `slides/5.html` continues with chatbot necessity, so slides 3 and 4 should feel visually and narratively connected.

**Chosen Direction:** Background-to-impact sequence

`slides/3.html` will shift from a generic "problem definition" layout to a "deployment background" layout. It will explain why customer-support teams are structurally ready for AI assistance:

1. response coverage gaps
2. repetitive inquiry overload
3. service-quality inconsistency
4. onboarding burden for new agents

`slides/4.html` will keep the impact dashboard and emphasize four high-signal outcomes that fit presentation pacing:

1. `+14%` overall support productivity uplift
2. `+34%` improvement for less-experienced agents
3. `-9%` agent attrition reduction
4. qualitative customer-experience improvement

**Content Rules:**
- Show a small source caption at the lower-left of `slides/4.html`.
- Phrase metrics as business outcomes for customer-support chatbot deployment.
- Keep the visual style aligned with nearby monochrome slides.

**Layout Notes:**
- `slides/3.html`
  - Left: eyebrow, title, accent bar, narrative copy
  - Right: one dark featured card plus supporting cards, mirroring slide 4
- `slides/4.html`
  - Left: explanatory copy plus small source caption at the lower-left
  - Right: one featured metric card plus three supporting cards
- Use concise Korean copy for presentation readability and maintain a matching grayscale dashboard style
